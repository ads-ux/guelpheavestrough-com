How Much Does Eavestrough Installation Cost in Guelph?

Published 2026-06-01 — Guelph Eavestrough

Eavestrough installation is one of the most impactful exterior investments for a Guelph home. Here's what the job actually costs in 2026.

Average Total Cost

Full eavestrough replacement on a typical Guelph home (1,500–2,000 sq ft) runs $800–$1,800 for seamless aluminum installation. Larger homes, complex rooflines, or two-storey sections push costs higher. Heritage homes with unique profiles or copper requirements are priced separately.

Cost Per Linear Foot

Seamless aluminum eavestrough in Guelph is typically priced at $8–$14 per linear foot for the trough itself, installed. Downspouts add $60–$120 each including installation. Most Guelph homes have 80–150 linear feet of eavestrough and 3–6 downspouts.

What Drives the Price Up

  • Fascia replacement: If the fascia board behind the eavestrough is rotted, it must be replaced before new eavestrough is installed. Add $8–$15 per linear foot for fascia.
  • Two-storey sections: Require taller ladders and additional setup time; typically add 15–25% to the relevant sections.
  • Steep pitch: Steeper roof pitches require higher-capacity troughs to handle water volume.
  • Colour matching: Standard aluminum colours (white, brown, beige) are stocked. Custom or matched colours may require ordering.
  • Gutter guards: Mesh or solid-cover leaf guards add $4–$12 per linear foot but can significantly reduce cleaning frequency.

Seamless vs. Sectional

Virtually all professional installations in Guelph use seamless aluminum, fabricated on-site from a roll of aluminum stock. Seamless troughs have no joints along the run (only at corners and downspout connections), which eliminates the leaking joints that are the primary failure point of older sectional systems. The cost premium over sectional is modest and well worth it for long-term performance.

How to Get an Accurate Quote

Eavestrough quotes require a site visit to measure linear footage, count downspout locations, assess fascia condition, and evaluate any access challenges. Phone estimates are rough approximations. Ask the contractor to note fascia condition explicitly in the quote — it's the most common source of unexpected additional cost.
