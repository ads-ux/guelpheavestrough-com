Eavestrough Repair Costs & Warning Signs

Most eavestrough repairs in Guelph fall into a handful of common issues. Isolated joint leaks are the most frequent call — resealing or patching a failed joint runs $100–$250. Sagging sections, usually caused by failed or under-spaced hangers rather than a problem with the trough itself, run $150–$350 to re-hang correctly. Downspout repair or unclogging runs $80–$200, and is worth addressing quickly since a blocked downspout backs water up the entire run.

Watch for these warning signs: sections pulling away from the fascia, visible cracks or holes, rust or peeling paint on aluminum or steel systems, water pooling near your foundation, or overflow during moderate rainfall (not just downpours). Any of these point to a repair need before the damage spreads to fascia, siding, or your foundation. If overflow only happens during heavy debris season, it may just need a cleaning — see how often eavestroughs should be cleaned in Guelph before assuming it's a structural issue.

Fascia condition is behind more eavestrough problems than people expect — a rotted board behind the trough can't hold hangers properly no matter how well the eavestrough itself is repaired. We check fascia condition on every repair call and repair it in the same visit when needed, typically $8–$15 per linear foot.

Repair or Replace? How to Tell

Repair is the right call for an isolated joint leak, a single sagging section, or a downspout problem — these are quick, targeted fixes. Full replacement starts to make more sense when your eavestrough is over 20 years old, showing rust or corrosion across multiple sections, undersized for your current roof, or failing in more than one place within the same season.

Weighing the two? See our eavestrough installation services if a full seamless replacement turns out to be the better value.
